The Rotation

This is a rotation-based program — not tied to specific days of the week. Train days shift based on your schedule, gym time, and energy. The order stays the same, rest days go where they need to.

The cycle:

→
B — Push + Delts
→
C — Pull + Biceps
→
Rest

D — Legs (Posterior)
→
Rest
→
A — repeat
Rules: Take a rest day when you need it — minimum 1 per cycle, ideally 2. Bryan sessions (~1x/month) slot in wherever they land and replace the nearest session or add on. Posing practice on rest days when energy allows.

Design Principles

Built from 9 weeks of your actual training data. Every exercise and weight is pulled from what you've been doing and progressing on.

1. Compounds don't change. Pendulum Squat 540, Cybex Press 450, DB RDL 180, HS Incline 220 — your money lifts stay.

2. Redundancy cut. 4 pulldown variations → 2. The ones where you're strongest. Same stimulus, less fatigue.

3. Arms absorbed. Triceps from pressing, biceps from pulling. No dedicated arm day needed.

4. Prep-aware. At 1,400-1,800 cal, recovery is the bottleneck. 19 intentional sets > 48 sets where the last 10 are junk.

5. Two distinct leg days. Session A = heavy quads (540 Pendulum). Session D = posterior focus (180 RDL, 450 Pendulum cap). Different stimulus, better recovery.
