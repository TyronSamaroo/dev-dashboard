Design Principles

Built from your data, not theory. Every exercise in this program is one you've been doing and progressing on. Nothing is random.

1. Compounds are king. Pendulum Squat 540, Cybex Press 450, DB RDL 180, HS Incline 220 — these are your money lifts. They stay.

2. Redundancy cut, not variety. You were doing 4 pulldown variations on pull day. Now it's 2 — the ones where you're strongest. Same stimulus, less fatigue.

3. Arms absorbed. Triceps get hit from all the pressing. Biceps get hit from all the pulling. Dedicated arm work is built into push/pull days instead of a separate session.

4. Prep-aware. As calories stay low, recovery is the bottleneck. Every set should be intentional. If you planned 19 sets and crushed them all, that's better than planning 48 and fading on the last 10.

5. Leg split is intentional. Monday = quad-heavy with Pendulum Squat at 540. Friday = hamstring-led with DB RDL at 180, Pendulum stays lighter at 450. Different stimulus, better recovery.
