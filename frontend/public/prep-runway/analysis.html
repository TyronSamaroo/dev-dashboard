Decision Board

The goal is not to show every field. It is to answer what to do next without losing the evidence trail.

Off? Anything off?

The off signal is digestion, not effort. The powder-only run was effective for scale, but the diarrhea-like stomach note after repeated shake boluses means whole-food protein should become the default base.

Meals Is meal timing consistent?

Mostly no. Meal one floated from late morning to early afternoon, and several days used big powder boluses. Thursday was cleaner: 11:34 whole-food meal, 1:50 pre-lift carb/protein, 6-7p post-lift protein.

Training Does workout load impact scale?

Yes. Thursday was two StairMaster sessions plus a dense lift and only produced a -0.3 lb Friday drop. That can be water/inflammation/gut noise, not proof the deficit stopped working.

Cardio Is StairMaster adapting down?

Not clearly. Current-week StairMaster output is still stable around 10-11 kcal/min with HR around 129-140. If burn falls, first check sleep, HR, and machine level before assuming adaptation.

Risk Where can this break?

Low calories + low fat + daily high-output sessions can break through sleep, GI, soreness, or cravings before the scale says anything useful. The warning is not one flat weigh-in; it is repeated bad markers.

Lever Next lever

Keep the aggressive setup if markers are good, but make it less fragile: whole-food protein first, one measured carb around lifting, and no third cardio block just to punish a flat scale.

What To Log Today

These are the missing data points that make tomorrow’s scale interpretation useful instead of emotional.

Body

GI/stool quality, soreness by body part, hunger, energy, and whether legs feel heavy on stairs.

Training

Lift split/body part, start/end, avg HR, max HR, Apple kcal, and whether sets felt normal or flat.

Cardio

StairMaster minutes, kcal, avg HR, max HR, and whether you chased burn or held effort steady.

Food

Meal windows, whole-food vs powder split, and any pre-lift carb/fat you added.
