Critical Commentary

  • Himalayan Pink Salt (Wednesday): 1,260mg in a single shot (Brian's pre-workout recommendation). That one item alone puts you at 63% of your daily 2,000mg sodium target. The performance benefit isn't worth the water retention it masks on the scale.
  • Flash IV Electrolyte: 500mg per serving × 3 uses = 1,500mg this week. That's nearly the entire daily target from electrolytes alone. Consider a low-sodium alternative (LMNT, plain water + potassium, or just rest-day hydration).
  • Chipotle Chicken (981mg): This is almost certainly third-party (HummusFit or similar). Self-cooked chicken breast is ~70mg sodium for 6oz. That's a 900mg difference per serving. Switching to self-cooked saves nearly 1,000mg per week if you use it again.
  • Turkey Bacon (400mg × 4 days = 1,600mg): It's in the Avocado Toast staple, so it hits every day. Even reducing to 1 slice (~200mg) cuts this in half. This is your most frequent high-sodium item.

Action Items

Priority Changes for Next Week

1 DROP the Himalayan Pink Salt — 1,260mg in one shot is not worth the pre-workout benefit. The water retention it creates negates any performance gain. Use a low-sodium electrolyte or just water instead.
2 Replace Flash IV → low-Na electrolyte or water. 500mg per serving × 3 uses = 1,500mg this week. Try LMNT (zero sodium, potassium-based), or just plain water + a potassium supplement on training days.
3 Replace Chipotle Chicken with self-cooked breast. Third-party = 981mg sodium. Self-cooked = ~70mg. That single swap saves 900mg per serving, or ~4,500mg per week if used 5 days a week.
4 Turkey Bacon: reduce to 1 slice or swap entirely. At 400mg per day it's adding ~2,800mg per week. One slice = 200mg = more manageable. Or switch to low-sodium bacon (e.g., Butterball or home-cooked pork belly).
5 Push protein to 180g. Currently averaging 168g, 12g short of target. Add half a scoop of protein powder to one meal. This also helps preserve muscle during the deficit.
6 Respect training volume caps. 51 sets on legs inflates scale weight for days. Stay under 23 sets for legs, 50 total per session. Quality over quantity — progressive overload wins contests, not volume.
